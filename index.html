Emotion Simulation System Overview

    
Our emotional simulation system models lifelike emotional behavior in virtual entities by combining psychological theory with real-time environmental and temporal responsiveness. Built on a hierarchical emotional architecture, the system simulates how emotions are generated, evolve, and interact—creating deeply immersive, adaptive, and human-like experiences.


    

      
At its core are eight primary emotions—such as Joy, Fear, and Anticipation—each with visual and behavioral parameters like intensity, stability, and contagion. From these, more complex emotional states emerge through interaction and combination, enabling rich emotional diversity. The system also models inter-emotional dynamics, such as how one emotion can amplify, suppress, or transform another, creating a responsive emotional network.

      
Environmental zones (like deserts or mountains) and temporal factors (such as time of day or season) shape emotional behavior in context-sensitive ways, making each experience feel grounded and dynamic. Additionally, an integrated memory system tracks emotional history, allowing virtual entities to learn, adapt, and evolve unique emotional personalities over time.

      
Whether used for AI-driven storytelling, adaptive game design, or emotional research, this simulation brings emotional intelligence to life in compelling, interactive environments.
